# TASK 5 – CYBERCRIME AWARENESS CASESTUDY & PREVENTION GUIDE

## PART B – Prevention Checklist

### Stay Safe Online

#### Stay Safe Online

- Do not accept friend requests from unknown profiles  
- Never perform banking transactions or log into sensitive accounts while on open cafeteria or station Wi-Fi  
- Log out of accounts when using shared or public computers (college labs, libraries, cyber cafes)  
- Enable 2FA on all accounts (Gmail, WhatsApp, LinkedIn) to add an extra layer beyond just a password  
- Check the URL before entering login credentials, look for "https://" and the correct domain name  
- Keep your phone's OS and apps updated, these help with patching of known security vulnerabilities. Do not install apps from unknown sources or third-party APK sites outside the Play Store or App Store  
- Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks like banking or logging into personal accounts. If you must use public Wi-Fi, use a VPN (Virtual Private Network) to encrypt your connection, ensuring your safety  
- Be wary of phishing emails that mimic official sources. It is always better to go to the official website directly  
- Report suspicious accounts, messages, or content on social platforms instead of just ignoring them  
- Do not reuse the same password across multiple platforms. Never save passwords in browsers on shared devices  
- Set UPI transaction limits (e.g., ₹5,000 daily) via app settings to minimize loss due to scam, if any  
- Never share UPI PIN, OTP, or card details with anyone, even if they claim to be bank support. No one asks for sensitive information of such sort  
- Enable biometric login (fingerprint/face ID) for UPI apps where available  
- Do not save your card details on e-commerce websites you rarely use  

- If you are a victim of cybercrime, immediately contact:  
  - National Crime Portal – https://cybercrime.gov.in/  
  - 24/7 Helpline Number: 1930
